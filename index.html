Interactive Proof

Prove that if for a relation $R$ we have $R^2 \subseteq R$, then we know that $R$ is transitive.

Writing $R^2$ is the same as writing $R \circ R$.

The relational composition $R \circ R$ is all pairs $(x,y)$ such that there exists a $z$ such that $xRzRy$.

Writing $xRzRy$ is the same as writing $xRz \wedge zRy$.

$xRz \wedge zRy$ means that $(x,z) \in R$ and $(z,y) \in R$. Since $(x,y) \in R^2$ and we know that $R^2 \subseteq R$, then we also know that $(x,y) \in R$.

So we know that whenever $(x,z)$ and $(z,y)$ are in $R$, $(x,y)$ is also in $R$.

Therefore, $R$ is transitive.
